
# Persona Profile — HR Manager (Talent Acquisition)

**Context:** HR Manager at a mid-sized tech company, responsible for talent acquisition in a rapidly growing environment. The company struggles to attract and retain top talent due to a lengthy, cumbersome recruitment process and low brand recognition.

---

## Snapshot
- **Role:** HR Manager (Talent Acquisition)
- **Org size / industry:** Mid-sized technology company
- **Primary mission:** Build and sustain a high-quality candidate pipeline and reduce time-to-fill for critical roles
- **Stakeholders:** Hiring managers, team leads, finance, legal, IT, external candidates, agencies

## Goals
- Shorten time-to-fill and time-to-slate
- Improve candidate quality and offer acceptance rate
- Create a consistent, candidate-friendly experience across roles and regions
- Strengthen employer brand visibility in target talent pools

## KPIs
- Time-to-fill (req open → offer accepted)
- Time-to-slate (req open → first qualified shortlist)
- Candidate drop-off rate per funnel stage
- Interview cycle time and panel utilization
- Offer acceptance rate
- Hiring manager satisfaction (post-hire CSAT)

## Pain Points (today)
- Cumbersome workflows (manual screening, scheduling, and feedback loops) leading to delays and drop-offs
- Low brand recognition in competitive markets, reducing inbound quality
- Fragmented tools (ATS, email, spreadsheets, chat) → context switching and missing data
- Inconsistent interview feedback; difficult to compare candidates quickly
- Limited visibility into pipeline health and bottlenecks

## Triggers (why act now)
- Rapid headcount growth and multiple critical, time-sensitive roles
- Leadership pressure to reduce costs and boost hiring speed/quality
- New markets/skill profiles where brand awareness is low

## Barriers / Risks
- Change management: recruiters and hiring managers used to current tools
- Data/privacy constraints around resumes, interviews, and feedback data
- Integration effort with ATS and calendar systems
- Risk of bias if AI usage is not governed and monitored

## What “Great” Looks Like
- Recruiters spend time on relationship-building, not admin
- Candidates experience timely, transparent, mobile-friendly journeys
- Hiring managers get clear shortlists, structured feedback, and fast decisions
- Leadership sees live dashboards for pipeline, DEI, and quality-of-hire
